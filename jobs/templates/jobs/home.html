Builder

I've always been interested in creating and improving things. My parents owned a bar when I was growing up and I learned early on how to use my hands to physically build things. Painting, light carpentry, holding the ladder, mopping the floors, adding a new extension so we could serve lunch - it was all about continuously improving the business. As we made changes, and tried new ideas, I came to really enjoy the process. Listening to customers, making changes, seeing the results and trying different things if they didn't work.

I didn't realize it until much later, but those early experiences of building the family business sparked something in me. I love building things. I enjoy not only the final finished product, but the thousand steps that got us there. Making sure those steps are repeatable - keeping track of what works and throwing out what doesn't.

Product Management is more than just a set of requirements and a deadline. It's living in the product, understanding the right questions to ask, rolling up your sleeves and doing any task necessary and being grateful for the people who you're lucky enough to have using your products.

I believe product leads, but it also serves. A product team should lead by example and serve with it's results.

Pacesetter

I belive in leading by example. I was lucky enough to have really good mentors early in my career that never lectured me but let me ask questions and learn from their example. I learned the value of taking personal pride in my work, not being afraid to do things I hadn't done before, and being open and honest with my boss. It's also really important for people on the team to see directors working as hard if not harder than they are.

In my experience, teams perform at their highest when they have clarity on the goals, are invested in the team's success, and feel supported by their team and the company. My role as a leader in an organization is to set up this framework so the teams can focus on the important work they're doing.

It's also important to be involved in the success and growth of individual team members. It's important as a manager to understand what motivates each team member, and what they're looking to get out of their job. I like to have weekly one on ones with team members, and weekly meetings with the teams on progress on our goals.
